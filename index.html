    If you are thinking about harming yourself, please call the National Suicide Prevention Hotline at

    1-800-273-8255.

    When you call the National Suicide Prevention Hotline, you will reach the crisis center in the Lifeline network closest to your location. After you call, you will hear a message saying you have reached the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline. You will hear hold music while your call is being routed. You will be helped by a skilled, trained crisis worker who will listen to your problems and will tell you about mental health services in your area. Your call is confidential and free.


    First Aid

    Call 911 right away if a person

    Is having difficulty breathing OR

    Collapses or is losing consciousness OR

    Has a seizure

    If you think someone has been poisoned, use PoisonHelp.org or call Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 right away. If a poisoning occurs, it’s important to react calmly and quickly to take appropriate first aid steps. Serious poisonings don’t always have early signs.

    BREATHED IN POISON?

    Get to fresh air right away
    Call Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 or use the Get Help tool for more treatment advice

    POISON IN THE EYES?

    Remove contact lenses if possible.
    Rinse the eye(s) with lukewarm running water.
    Call Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 or use the Get Help tool for more treatment advice.

    SWALLOWED POISON?

    Gently wipe out the mouth and give a small amount of water.
    Do not try home remedies or try to make someone throw up.
    Call Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 or use the Get Help tool for more treatment advice.

    POISON ON THE SKIN?

    Take off any clothing that the poison has touched.
    Rinse skin with running water.
    Call Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 or use the Get Help tool for more treatment advice.
